## Research & Statistics (Sources)

### Muslim Digital Behavior
- **81%** of Muslims use mobile devices during Ramadan (TGM Ramadan Survey)
- **66%** of Muslims in MENA, Asia, Europe, Africa plan to use Islamic apps during Ramadan
- **1.6x** spike in Islamic app downloads during Ramadan (Pakistan sample)
- **52–53%** of highly religious Americans use apps for scripture/religious info (Pew)
- **28%** use apps for prayer reminders (Pew)

### Muslim Demographics (USA)
- **~3.45M** Muslims in US (2017); projected **8.1M (2.1%)** by 2050
- **~1/3** of Muslim adults under 30 (younger than general population)
- **~75%** immigrants or children of immigrants
- **31%** college graduates (comparable to general public)

### Sleep & Wellness
- **34%** have trouble falling asleep; **29%** struggle staying asleep 3+ nights/week
- **57%** cite stress, **46%** anxiety as top causes of poor sleep
- **88%** with good sleep satisfaction report “flourishing” vs **47%** with poor sleep
- **36%** of US adults diagnosed with a sleep disorder (AASM 2024)

### Privacy Concerns
- **FTC 2024**: Major apps engage in “vast surveillance,” collect billions of location data points
- Location data can reveal visits to **religious organizations**, medical facilities, homes
- Users struggle to manage privacy; awareness of data collection is limited

## Persona 1: Fatima — The Revert Building Habits

### Demographics
- **Age:** 22–35
- **Location:** USA, UK, Canada (Western diaspora)
- **Role:** New Muslim (revert), often 1–3 years in Islam
- **Tech:** Heavy smartphone use; prefers mobile-first content

### Goals
- Learn the Sunnah bedtime routine step-by-step
- Understand *why* each practice matters (not just what to do)
- Build habits without overwhelm
- Have trustworthy, verifiable sources to share with mentors

### Pain Points
- Overwhelmed by long articles and conflicting info
- Worried about weak/fabricated hadith
- No consistent accountability; forgets steps
- Wants to “do it right” but doesn’t know where to start

### Behaviors
- Searches: “how to sleep like the prophet,” “Sunnah before bed,” “Islamic bedtime routine”
- Consumes: Short videos, carousels, step-by-step guides
- Shares: With revert support groups, mentors
- Retention signal: Returns to checklist, uses Wudu guide, checks Hadith tooltips

### Statistics That Resonate
- Many reverts cite lack of guidance as a barrier to consistent practice
- Reverts often seek authentic sources (Sunnah.com links matter)
- Short, structured content performs better for habit-building

### SunnahSleep Fit
- Checklist with Hadith tooltips and Sunnah.com links
- Wudu guide with transliteration
- No account = low commitment, easy to try
- Transliteration for recitations (Ayat al-Kursi, Three Quls)

### Content Angles
- “5 Sunnah steps before sleep (with sources)”
- “What the Prophet ﷺ did every night”
- “New to Islam? Start here”
- Educational carousels with hadith references

## Persona 2: Ahmed — The Busy Professional

### Demographics
- **Age:** 28–45
- **Location:** Urban, global (high Muslim professional diaspora)
- **Role:** Full-time worker; often parent
- **Tech:** Uses productivity and wellness apps; time-poor

### Goals
- Pray Isha on time and wake for Fajr consistently
- Track sleep and prayer adherence
- Get reliable alarms without manual calculation
- See progress (streaks, stats) for motivation

### Pain Points
- Works late; misses Isha or sleeps through Fajr
- Forgets bedtime steps; no accountability
- Generic sleep apps lack Islamic context
- Needs location-based prayer times

### Behaviors
- Searches: “Fajr alarm,” “wake for Fajr,” “Isha prayer time,” “sleep tracker”
- Uses: Prayer apps, productivity tools, habit trackers
- Retention signal: Sets alarms, checks Sleep tab daily, uses streak counter

### Statistics That Resonate
- 28% of highly religious Americans use prayer reminder apps
- Sleep quality linked to life satisfaction (88% vs 47%)
- Stress (57%) and anxiety (46%) are top sleep disruptors—routine helps

### SunnahSleep Fit
- Prayer alarms (Fajr, Isha, Tahajjud, “X min before Fajr”)
- Sleep tracker with Isha/Fajr adherence %
- Streak counter for accountability
- Location auto-detect for prayer times

### Content Angles
- “Never miss Fajr again”
- “Track your Isha & Fajr streak”
- “The 5-minute Sunnah before bed”
- Stats-based: “Consistency beats perfection”

## Persona 3: Aisha — The Tahajjud Seeker

### Demographics
- **Age:** 25–40
- **Location:** Global; often active in Islamic communities
- **Role:** Spiritually motivated; seeks night worship
- **Tech:** Uses Islamic apps, follows scholars and educators

### Goals
- Wake in the last third of the night for Tahajjud
- Calculate Tahajjud time accurately for her location
- Get a gentle, reliable wake-up
- Maintain Qailulah to support night worship

### Pain Points
- Manual calculation of last third is tedious
- Generic alarms don’t adapt to season/location
- Needs to sleep early to wake for Tahajjud

### Behaviors
- Searches: “Tahajjud time,” “last third of night,” “when to pray Tahajjud”
- Engages with Islamic educators, scholars, content on night prayer
- Retention signal: Enables Tahajjud alarm, uses Qailulah reminder, completes bedtime checklist

### Statistics That Resonate
- Split vs consolidated sleep: studies show both can work when total sleep is maintained
- Sleep architecture around Fajr is a studied topic (NCBI, sleep journals)
- Qailulah (power nap) supports night worship readiness

### SunnahSleep Fit
- Tahajjud alarm (calculates last third from Maghrib–Fajr)
- Qailulah reminder
- “30 min before Fajr” alarm
- Location-based prayer times

### Content Angles
- “When does Allah descend? The last third of the night”
- “Tahajjud made simple”
- “Qailulah: the Sunnah nap that prepares you for night prayer”
- Hadith: Bukhari 1145 (Allah descends)

## Persona 4: Yusuf — The Privacy-Conscious User

### Demographics
- **Age:** 25–50
- **Location:** Global; often tech-savvy or security-aware
- **Role:** Values privacy; skeptical of data collection
- **Tech:** Uses privacy tools; avoids apps that require login/tracking

### Goals
- Islamic content without account or tracking
- No ads, no data sharing
- Works offline
- Transparency about what the app does with data

### Pain Points
- Many apps require login, collect location, sell data
- FTC 2024: “Vast surveillance” by major apps
- Location data can reveal visits to mosques, religious spaces
- Distrust of free apps (“if it’s free, you’re the product”)

### Behaviors
- Searches: “privacy-focused Muslim app,” “Islamic app no account”
- Reads: Privacy policies before install
- Retention signal: Uses app without creating account; prefers PWA/offline

### Statistics That Resonate
- FTC report: apps collect billions of location data points
- Data can reveal religious organization visits
- Users struggle to control or understand data collection

### SunnahSleep Fit
- 100% local storage; no account
- No tracking, no analytics, no ads
- PWA; works offline
- Privacy policy clearly states data stays on device

### Content Angles
- “Your sleep data never leaves your phone”
- “No account. No tracking. No ads.”
- “Why we built SunnahSleep for privacy”
- “Islamic content without the surveillance”
